Case Study: Cyberbullying

Cyberbullying is a form of online harassment where individuals are targeted through social media, messaging apps, or other digital platforms. It includes spreading rumors, posting hurtful comments, sharing embarrassing content, or sending threatening messages.

A typical case begins when a student posts something online, such as a photo or opinion. A group of peers may start making negative comments or memes targeting that individual. Gradually, the harassment increases—fake accounts may be created to send abusive messages or spread false information. In some cases, private photos or chats are shared without consent. The victim may feel pressured, isolated, and unable to escape, as the bullying continues across multiple platforms.

Teenagers and college students are the most common targets, especially those who are active on social media or perceived as different. Victims often hesitate to report incidents due to fear of embarrassment or retaliation.

The consequences of cyberbullying can be severe. It may lead to emotional distress, anxiety, loss of self-confidence, and even depression. Academic performance can also be affected. In extreme cases, it can result in long-term psychological harm.

Reference: UNICEF (Cyberbullying Awareness Resources); Government of India Cyber Crime Portal (cybercrime.gov.in).
